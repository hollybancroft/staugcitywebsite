History

Click on a picture to see the history!



St. Augustine, Florida was founded in the year 1565 by Spanish settlers. It was founded in an attempt to claim the Florida territory for Spain, in the midst of French settlement.

Much of the original buildings and architectural structures have been destroyed due to the city being invaded by the British in 1702. However, there are still thirty-six buildings preserved from the Colonial era, and the street layouts reflect a Spanish style of engineering.

The Castillo de San Marcos is a most impressive structure of history that remains in St. Augustine. It is a military fort that was completed by the Spanish in the late seventeeth century. It is one of the most popular historical landmarks in St. Augustine.

St. Augustine was transformed into a winter vacation destination by Henry Flagler two decades after the Civil War. Flagler connected St. Augustine to the rest of the east coast by railway, and reconstructed St. Augustine with it's now iconic architectural style. He also built three famous hotels, one of which was transformed into what is now Flagler college.
